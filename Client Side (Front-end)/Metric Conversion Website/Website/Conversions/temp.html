Temprature Conversion Description

Temperature is a numerical representation of hot or cold compared against baselines, typically the point at which water freezes and boils.

In most in instances, temperature measures how much heat there is in a particular entity —in a liquid, such as water, or a solid, such as soil. In meteorology, temperature measures the heat content of the atmosphere.

The instrument used to measure temperature is a thermometer, a word derived from the Greek thermos, meaning "hot," and metron, "measure."
